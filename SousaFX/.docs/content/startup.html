Startup¶

Here’s how to start up SousaFX and SousaPlayback.

SousaFX¶

Steps 2 - 5 are only necessary the first time SousaFX starts.

  1. Open ~/Documents/​Max 8/​Projects/​sousastep/​SousaFX/​SousaFX.maxproj. SousaFX’s Startup Preferences window will appear.
  1. Click on menubar > Settings > Audio Status... and set your preferred driver, sample rate, and vector size. 48 kHz and 128 samples are recommended. If the audio crackles during use then try a vector size of 256, or 512.
  1. Close the Audio Status window and select your preferred audio interface in SousaFX’s Startup Preferences.
  2. Uncheck the Startup Preferences’ pause button. SousaFX will appear in 2 - 5 minutes.
  1. Once SousaFX is running, open the Audio IO Status window and ensure that the Main and Monitor outputs are routed to the audio interface’s Main and Headphone/Aux outputs, respectively.

From here, you can use SousaFX on its own, or you can run it alongside SousaPlayback.

SousaPlayback¶

Steps 7 - 9 are only necessary the first time SousaPlayback starts.

  1. Open ~/Documents/​Max 8/​Projects/ ​sousastep/​default/ ​SousaPlayback template Project/ ​SousaPlayback template.als.
  2. If the Black Octopus Drum Loops are installed then drum clips will be available in session view, but if they’re missing then feel free to delete them and add your own.
  3. Ensure that the Main and Monitor return tracks are routed to the audio interface’s Main and Headphone/Aux outputs, respectively.
  4. Set SousaFX’s Tempo Source to “Ableton’s Tempo”.
  5. Enable Link by clicking its button on the far left of Live’s control bar.
  6. Select the first scene and hit enter.
